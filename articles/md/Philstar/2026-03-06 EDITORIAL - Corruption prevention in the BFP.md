# EDITORIAL - Corruption prevention in the BFP

#### March 6, 2026 | 12:00am

As the nation observes Fire Prevention Month, attention is focused on officials and personnel of the Bureau of Fire Protection who face investigation on corruption-related charges.

Secretary Jonvic Remulla of the Department of the Interior and Local Government has reportedly recommended to President Marcos the replacement of Jesus Fernandez as BFP chief.

Fernandez has not responded so far to Remulla’s accusation that the BFP director, as head of the bureau’s bids and awards committee, had received P14.75 million in kickbacks from the procurement of 132 ambulances.

Charges of direct bribery, malversation of public funds and conspiracy were filed last Wednesday against Fernandez before the Office of the Ombudsman. Charged along with Fernandez is supplier Rosaverna Sangga.

A probe conducted by the DILG showed that Fernandez met with the supplier in November 2024 in a restaurant in Quezon City, during which he allegedly received a suitcase containing P6 million and a bag with P4.75 million.

During a second meeting in September last year, Fernandez allegedly received luggage containing P4 million.

The amounts were allegedly in exchange for a joint venture with an automotive company that became the only one to qualify for the bidding requirements for the ambulances.

Remulla noted that Fernandez owns expensive houses with swimming pools in Laguna and Cavite. The DILG chief is also looking into reports of anomalies in the BFP’s recruitment process and in carrying out fire safety inspections.

Complaints about abuses in the BFP have been aired for many years now, notably in the conduct of fire safety inspections. A common complaint among entrepreneurs is being forced to replace fire extinguishers every year, and to buy the replacements from a particular supplier before fire safety clearances are issued.

Remulla is seeking the preventive suspension of Fernandez and is moving for a thorough overhaul of the BFP.

The scandal in the bureau has overshadowed what should be the focus of this special month: fire prevention. There have been improvements over the years in terms of equipment, but firefighting capability all over the country remains inadequate.

People also continue to be largely unmindful of fire prevention measures, such as periodic inspections of electrical fixtures. In informal communities, people still leave lighted candles unattended.

The BFP is tasked to ensure compliance with fire safety standards in commercial and business districts as well as government buildings. The bureau can’t carry out its mandate if its officials are busy looking for opportunities for self-enrichment.
