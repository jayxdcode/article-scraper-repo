# EDITORIAL — Deadly overloading

#### The Philippine Star

#### February 23, 2026 | 12:00am

![featured](https://media.philstar.com/photos/2023/05/30/editorialdefault-1_2023-05-30_13-49-27.jpg)

[https://www.philstar.com/opinion/2026/02/23/2509813/editorial-deadly-overloading](https://www.philstar.com/opinion/2026/02/23/2509813/editorial-deadly-overloading)

Skepticism greeted the announcement of the Philippine Coast Guard that the roll on-roll off vessel Trisha Kerstin 3 was not overloaded when it set sail for Jolo, Sulu on the night of Jan. 25 from the Zamboanga City port.

Hours later on Jan. 26, the 31-year-old Ro-Ro ship sank off Baluk-Baluk Island in Basilan. PCG personnel said the vessel had 332 passengers and 27 crew members – still within its authorized capacity.

But that was the number recorded on the ship’s manifest. As of yesterday, 65 bodies had been found, with 14 people still missing, while 293 were recorded as survivors. That makes 372, over the authorized capacity.

In addition, some of the survivors narrated details indicating that rolling cargo on the Ro-Ro appeared to weigh more than the allowed capacity, causing the ship to list even before it set sail. Motor vehicles were reportedly not weighed before these were allowed to roll into the ship.

In almost all cases of ships sinking in Philippine waters, the precise number of passengers is not known.

PCG officials explained that while they observe strict protocols in keeping accurate passenger manifests, this is possible only in established ports. And they have no control over passengers boarding at sea – a practice that remains prevalent in this archipelago of 7,641 islands.

The entire fleet of Aleson Shipping Lines, owner of the Trisha Kerstin 3, has been grounded. The company, where the wife of Zamboanga City Mayor Kyhmer Olaso is a part-owner, may lose its certificate of public convenience and face administrative charges, according to the Department of Transportation.

The DOTr is also investigating three officers of the Maritime Industry Authority behind the issuance of safety certificates to the Trisha Kerstin 3. The PCG, which is conducting a separate probe, has placed six of its personnel under preventive suspension.

Whether any of these moves will lead to significant penalties remains to be seen. Shipping operators have largely gotten off with a slap on the wrist in the numerous maritime disasters that have led to thousands of deaths in the country over the past decades.

For many Filipinos, passenger ferries and Ro-Ro vessels are more affordable alternatives to air travel, and in many areas, the only modes for reaching islands with no land or air connectivity.

Authorities must tighten regulation of sea travel, through better enforcement of safety rules as well as the imposition of stiff penalties on violators. Getting away with infractions that lead to deaths has bred impunity.
